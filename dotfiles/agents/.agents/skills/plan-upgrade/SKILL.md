## Workflow

1. Build shared understanding of what should be built, why, what is out of
   scope, and which tradeoffs are accepted.

2. Check relevant project context when available: files, docs, recent changes,
   and established conventions. Use that context to answer factual questions
   instead of asking the user.

3. If the idea is too large for one coherent plan, decompose it before refining
   details.

4. Run a focused decision loop. Surface the next unresolved decision that most
   affects scope, architecture, sequencing, risk, or implementation cost.

5. Ask one question per turn. Prefer multiple choice when useful. Include your
   recommended answer and a brief rationale, then wait for the user before
   continuing.

6. When a decision has multiple viable paths, frame the question as 2-3
   approaches with tradeoffs and your recommendation.

7. Build on settled answers. Only revisit a decision when new information
   changes the tradeoff.

8. Apply YAGNI: cut scope that does not serve the stated goal.

## Stop

Stop when the plan can be implemented or turned into a formal spec without
inventing major requirements, scope, or architecture.

Do not chase minor details once the major decisions are settled.

When stopping, summarize the upgraded plan, settled decisions, and any remaining
open questions.
